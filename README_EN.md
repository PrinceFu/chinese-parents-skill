## Framework Overview

### 5 Mental Models

| Model | One-liner | Best For |
|-------|-----------|----------|
| Education = sole upward mobility channel | From imperial exams to college entrance exams to school district housing — "studying" is a millennia-old faith in social mobility | Analyzing education anxiety, school district housing, tutoring |
| Children = core family investment | Children are not independent individuals but appreciating assets of family wealth | Understanding career/marriage/fertility intervention |
| Self-sacrifice = moral authority | Parental sacrifice creates an unpayable "moral debt" | Decoding "Everything I do is for your own good" |
| Collective anxiety > individual rationality | "What others do" is the core reference for decisions | Analyzing conformity, theater effect |
| Blood relation = no boundaries | No privacy or independent personhood in the parent-child relationship | Understanding boundary violations, mama's boy phenomenon |

### 8 Decision Heuristics

Economic leverage control · Comparative driving · Future catastrophizing · Emotional withdrawal threat · Delayed gratification false promises · Experience authority suppression · Rhetorical question ending discussion · Conformity safety strategy

### 7 Expression DNA Patterns

Authority assumption · Suffering narrative · Comparative anxiety · Outcome orientation · Negation first · Boundary blurring · Future threat

### Special Expression Systems

**Food as "I love you"**: "Have you eaten?" = I miss you · "Eat more, you're too thin" = I worry about you · "I'm not hungry, you eat" = I'm willing to sacrifice for you

**"Come eat" = Chinese pseudo-apology**: Chinese parents rarely say "sorry" — they use actions instead of words.

## Honest Boundaries

This framework **can**:
- Provide analytical tools for understanding Chinese-style parenting behavior
- Help decode the deep logic behind parental behavior
- Offer frameworks and material for writing, research, and dialogue

This framework **cannot**:
- Replace psychological counseling — seek professional help for serious psychological trauma
- Predict individual differences — "Chinese parents" is a cultural pattern, with enormous variation across families
- Judge "right or wrong" — the framework presents patterns and tensions, not moral verdicts
- Replace real conversations — understanding the framework is not the same as understanding your parents
